2021 Call for Submissions: #MyPandemicStory

Terms and Conditions

  1. Eligibility

    The Royal Ontario Museum (the “ROM”) is accepting work of art submissions to be considered for a ROM-original exhibition (the “Exhibition”). The submission process is open to all legal residents of Ontario, Canada. All Submitters must be between 4 to 18 years of age. Children 17 and under must have the consent of a parent or legal guardian in order to submit a work.

  2. How to Submit

    You may submit your original work between 12:00:01 a.m. Eastern Time (“ET”) on April 6, 2021 and 11:59:59 p.m. ET on June 27, 2021 (the “Submission Period”). To enter a submission:

    1. Submit a photo or video of your work of art through the online submission form at projects.rom.on.ca/mypandemicstory. Submitters must complete all data requested on the submission form.
    2. Share a photo or video of your work of art from your public unlocked Twitter or Instagram account and include @ROMtoronto and #MyPandemicStory. Instagram stories will not be accepted as a submission. Children under the age of 13 will require a parent/guardian to submit the work on behalf of the child.

    Eligible submissions must show “what life has been like for you during the pandemic” as the subject matter; it could show you in a certain moment or setting, it could be you expressing a feeling, or it could represent something important from your pandemic world. Eligible works include but are not limited to:

    • a drawing, painting, collage, photograph, or other type of picture;
    • a video or song, poem or written piece;
    • a sculpture or anything 3-dimensional.

    The following types of files can be accepted: JPG, PNG, TIF, SVG, PDF, AVI, FLV, MOV, MP4, MPG, WMV. Photos and videos will be collected and displayed at rom.on.ca/en/mypandemicstory.

    Only one Submission will be accepted per person. Entries submitted by any means other than those described above, or not received during the Submission Period, will not be accepted and are not eligible. The ROM reserves the right to disqualify Submissions not fulfilling any aspect of these Official Terms and Conditions, or works submitted by ineligible persons, as solely determined by the ROM.

  3. Display of Submissions

    By submitting a work of art, all Submitters consent to the use of their work, name, age, city of residence, e-mail address, telephone number, comments, description, and image or video without further notice or compensation, whether on videotape, photograph or any other means, for the administration of this Exhibition and any and all publicity carried out by the ROM, including on the ROM’s website, e-communications and other promotional materials related to the Exhibition, and on the ROM premises in connection with the Exhibition. The ROM makes no commitment to display, publicize or credit any particular Submitter or work.

  4. Selection

    The submissions will be reviewed and works selected to be included in the Exhibition will be determined by a panel appointed by the ROM for the Exhibition (the “Panel”). The Panel will select works for inclusion in the Exhibition based on the theme of “what life has been like for you during the pandemic”. The ROM may, at any time and in its sole discretion, change the composition of the Panel or the number of individuals forming the Panel. All decisions of the Panel are final and binding and not subject to appeal or audit.

    Selected submissions will be required to comply with all other Terms and Conditions, all in the sole discretion of the ROM.

    If your work is selected to be displayed in the Exhibition, the ROM will contact you to arrange for your original piece to be shipped to the Museum and will return the piece to you once the Exhibition is complete; at no cost to you. Some of the pieces from the Exhibition may be considered for inclusion in the Museum’s collection.

  5. Contacting Selected Submissions

The ROM will make up to three (3) attempts to contact the Selected Submitters.
